// Regulatory & Tax Context

  • CTT (Commodity Transaction Tax) = 0.01% on the SELL side only, introduced July 2013. It is non-deductible against trading losses for salaried individuals.
  • Income Tax on futures — MCX futures P&L is treated as non-speculative business income under Section 43(5) of the Income Tax Act. Taxed at your full slab rate.
  • Losses are deductible — futures losses can be set off against other business income (not salary) and carried forward for 8 years.
  • CTT is paid on losses too — if you enter a trade and exit at a loss, CTT is still charged on the sell value. The ₹150 walks out regardless.
  • Stamp duty varies slightly by state. The 0.002% rate shown is the Central Stamp Duty applicable since June 2020.
  • GST 18% applies only to brokerage and exchange transaction charges — not on CTT, stamp duty, or SEBI fees.
  • Scalping MCX Gold is structurally unprofitable for retail — the minimum viable move after all friction is ₹200–300/10g. Trade the multi-day trend, not the noise.
  • SEBI turnover fee = ₹10 per crore of turnover (both sides counted). Very small but worth knowing.
