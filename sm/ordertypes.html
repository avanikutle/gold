Entry / Basic Orders
9 types
Market Order
MKT
Entry
Execute immediately at the best available price. No price guarantee — speed is the priority. Used when you must enter/exit regardless of price.
Entry conditionImmediate
Execution priceBest available (₹99–₹101 range)
SL / TargetMust be placed separately
PlatformsAll brokers
⚡ Gap risk: fills at any price — slippage uncontrolled
Guaranteed fillNo price controlFastest entry
Limit Order
LMT
Entry
Buy only at ₹100 or below; Sell only at ₹100 or above. Rests in order book until matched. Perfect for entering at your exact price.
Entry conditionPrice ≤ ₹100 (for buy)
Execution price₹100 or better
SL / TargetMust be placed separately
PlatformsAll brokers
⚠ May not fill if price never reaches ₹100
Price controlFill not guaranteedQueue-dependent
After Market Order
AMO
Entry
Placed after market close (3:30 PM) for execution next session at open. Useful for pre-planning overnight positions. Available as Market or Limit type.
Placement window3:45 PM – 8:57 AM (next day)
ExecutionOpening of next session
Order typeCan be Market or Limit AMO
PlatformsZerodha, Groww, Dhan, AngelOne
ℹ Executed at pre-open auction price or market open
Pre-plan entriesNext-day fill
Immediate or Cancel
IOC
Entry
Executes immediately — fully or partially. Any unfilled quantity is cancelled instantly. Never sits in the order book. Useful for scalping or when you want partial fills at your price.
Entry conditionImmediate execution attempt
Unmatched qtyCancelled at once
Partial fillYes — accepted
PlatformsAll NSE/BSE brokers
ℹ Zero queue time — it fills or it dies immediately
Partial fills OKNo queue waitMay fill 0 lots
Fill or Kill
FOK
Entry
Must fill the entire order quantity immediately or the whole order is cancelled. No partial fills allowed. Rarely available on retail platforms — more common in institutional trading.
Entry conditionFull qty must fill at once
Partial fillNot permitted — cancels entirely
Use caseLarge block orders, algos
PlatformsLimited retail support (API mostly)
ℹ Stricter than IOC — all or nothing, instantly
No partialsAll-or-nothing
Market on Open
MOO
Entry
Executes at the opening price of the trading session. Submitted before open; fills during the opening auction at whatever clearing price emerges. Popular with gap traders.
TimingPre-market → fills at open
PriceOpening auction price
Guaranteed fillYes (at open price)
PlatformsVia AMO (Market type) or API
⚡ Opening gaps can cause large slippage vs previous close
Guaranteed open fillPrice unknown pre-open
Limit on Open
LOO
Entry
Like MOO but with a price limit at open. Will fill at open only if the opening auction price is at or below your limit (for buy). Otherwise cancelled. Protects against large gap-ups at open.
TimingPre-market → fills only if open ≤ limit
PriceOpening price or better
Cancel conditionIf open > limit price (for buy)
PlatformsAMO Limit type / API
⚠ May not fill if stock gaps above your limit on open
Open price protectionMay cancel if gap
Market on Close
MOC
Entry
Executes at the closing price of the session (closing auction 3:00–3:30 PM on NSE). Used by mutual funds, index rebalancers, and BTST traders to enter/exit at official close.
TimingClosing auction window
PriceOfficial closing auction price
Guaranteed fillYes (at closing price)
PlatformsZerodha (CNC), institutional API
ℹ Price known only after close. BTST-friendly.
Official close priceDelay vs intraday
Limit on Close
LOC
Entry
Like MOC but only fills if the closing auction price matches your limit. Protects against adverse closing auctions. Niche — mainly API/institutional.
TimingClosing auction
PriceClose price or better (limit floor)
Cancel conditionIf close > limit (for buy)
PlatformsMainly API / institutional
ℹ Combines price protection with close-price targeting
Close + limit protectionMay not fill
Order Validity Types
5 types
Day Order
DAY
Validity
Order is valid only for the current trading session. If not filled by 3:30 PM, it is automatically cancelled. Default validity for most retail orders in India.
Valid tillMarket close same day
Auto-cancel3:30 PM if unfilled
Default forAll intraday trades
PlatformsAll brokers (default)
ℹ Safest validity — position won't linger overnight
Auto-expiresDefault choice
Good Till Cancelled
GTC
Validity
Order remains active across sessions until filled OR manually cancelled. NSE/BSE allow GTC for a maximum of 1 year. Critical for swing traders who want to set and forget at a level.
Valid tillFilled or manually cancelled (max 1 yr)
Survives weekendsYes
Use caseLimit orders at key support/resistance
PlatformsZerodha, Dhan, Fyers (via GTT)
⚠ Review periodically — stale orders can fill at bad prices
Multi-dayManual cancel needed
Good Till Date
GTD
Validity
Order stays active until a specific date you choose. After that date, it cancels automatically. More controlled than GTC — useful for event-driven trades (Budget, RBI policy).
Valid tillUser-specified date (auto-cancels)
Survives weekendsYes
Use caseEvent-driven setups with a deadline
PlatformsDhan, Fyers, API
ℹ Combines patience of GTC with the safety of an expiry
Auto-expires on dateEvent trades
Good Till Triggered
GTT
Validity
A conditional order that waits — days, weeks, months — until a price trigger is hit. When triggered, it places a Limit order automatically. Zerodha's GTT also supports two-leg (entry + SL) configurations.
Trigger price₹100 (your entry condition)
Action on triggerPlaces a Limit order at ₹100
Max validity1 year
PlatformsZerodha (Kite), Groww, Dhan
✓ Stored on broker server — survives app restarts
Cross-sessionServer-storedTrigger → Limit only
Disclosed Quantity
DQ / Iceberg
Validity
Show only a portion of your total order to the market. E.g., you want to buy 100 lots but show only 10 at a time. Reduces market impact. Requires minimum disclosed qty to be ≥ 10% of total on NSE.
Total qty100 lots (hidden)
Shown qty10 lots (visible in L2)
Refill ruleAuto-reloads each time slice fills
PlatformsZerodha (API), institutional desks
ℹ NSE rule: disclosed qty ≥ 10% of order qty
Hides intentAPI/Pro platforms
Risk / Stop Orders
5 types
Stop-Loss Market
SLM
Risk
When price hits your trigger (₹90), a Market order fires. Guarantees exit but not price. In a fast crash or circuit filter, you may exit at ₹87 or worse.
Trigger₹90
Execution typeMarket — best available
Guaranteed exitYes (at some price)
PlatformsAll brokers
⚡ No price floor — can exit at ₹87, ₹85 on fast moves
Always exitsPrice unknown
Stop-Loss Limit
SL / SL-L
Risk
Trigger at ₹90 fires a Limit order at ₹88. Protects from excessive slippage but risks non-execution if price gaps below ₹88 without touching it.
Trigger₹90
Limit price₹88 (floor)
Guaranteed exitNo — may stay stuck
PlatformsAll brokers
⚡ Gap below ₹88 = ORDER STAYS OPEN — you remain in position
Price floorMay not fill on gap
Trailing Stop Loss
TSL
Risk
SL moves up automatically as price rises, always trailing by ₹10. Locks in profit progressively. Never moves down. ₹100 entry → SL at ₹90 → price ₹130 → SL now ₹120.
Trail distance₹10 (fixed absolute)
SL at ₹130 price₹120
SL at ₹150 price₹140
PlatformsDhan (SUPER), Fyers, API algos
✓ Profit compounds automatically — ideal for trending moves
Auto profit lockNever moves downMay exit early on whipsaw
One Cancels Other
OCO
Risk
Two orders linked: if SL (₹90) fires, Target (₹130) is automatically cancelled — and vice versa. Standard risk management pairing. Bracket Order is the retail implementation of OCO.
Order A (SL)SLM at ₹90
Order B (Target)Limit Sell at ₹130
When A firesB is cancelled
PlatformsVia Bracket Order / SUPER / API
✓ No double-exit risk — exactly one leg will execute
Auto-cancel of otherClean exit logic
One Triggers Other
OTO
Risk
Parent order triggers a child order upon fill. E.g., Limit Buy at ₹100 fills → automatically places SLM at ₹90. Eliminates the gap between entry and SL placement. Mainly via API or platforms like Dhan SUPER.
ParentLimit Buy at ₹100
Child (triggered on fill)SLM at ₹90
Trigger conditionParent fully/partially filled
PlatformsDhan, API (Zerodha/Fyers)
✓ SL appears the moment entry fills — zero manual delay
Zero SL lagPlatform-dependent
Combination / Multi-Leg Orders
5 types
Bracket Order
BO
Combo
Three-legged order: Entry + SL + Target placed simultaneously. Whichever of SL/Target hits first cancels the other. Margin benefit on some platforms. Entry must be Market or Limit.
EntryMarket/Limit at ₹100
SL legSLM at ₹90 (auto-placed)
Target legLimit Sell at ₹130 (auto-placed)
PlatformsZerodha, Dhan, Fyers, AngelOne
✓ OCO logic built in — cleanest retail combo order
All 3 legs at onceLower marginIntraday only (most brokers)
Cover Order
CO
Combo
Entry + mandatory SLM. The SL cannot be removed once placed — broker enforces it. In return, you get significantly reduced margin (leverage). No target leg (unlike BO).
EntryMarket/Limit at ₹100
SL legSLM at ₹90 (mandatory, irremovable)
Target legNone — manual exit required
PlatformsZerodha, Upstox, AngelOne
⚠ SL is locked — can't be moved lower, only higher
Lowest marginNo target automationIntraday only
One Triggers OCO
OTOCO
Combo
A three-step conditional flow: if your entry triggers → places an OCO (SL + Target pair). The full BO/SUPER logic replicated via API. Most retail Bracket Orders are OTOCO under the hood.
Step 1Limit Entry triggers at ₹100
Step 2 (on fill)OCO created: SLM ₹90 + Limit ₹130
Step 3Whichever fires → other cancels
PlatformsAPI / Dhan SUPER / Advanced BO
✓ Fully automated 3-stage trade lifecycle
Full automationAPI/advanced platforms
SUPER Order
SUPER (Dhan)
Combo
Dhan's proprietary 5-in-1 order: Entry Trigger + Limit Entry + SL + Target + Trail Jump. All legs stored on Dhan servers, hidden from exchange until each activates. The most advanced retail order in India.
Trigger price₹100
SL₹90 with Trail Jump ₹10
Target₹130
Trail logicSL steps up ₹10 for each ₹10 rise
✓ Server-side — survives internet disconnection
5 legs in 1Trail built-inServer-stored
Basket Order
BASKET
Combo
Multiple orders for different symbols placed simultaneously in one click. Used for index replication, multi-stock strategies, or portfolio rebalancing. Not a single-instrument order — operates across many.
ExampleBuy NIFTY Fut + Sell BANKNIFTY Fut simultaneously
Order types insideEach leg can be Market or Limit
Use casePairs trading, hedging, index replication
PlatformsZerodha, Dhan, Fyers, AngelOne
ℹ Each leg executes independently — timing slippage between legs
Multi-symbol at onceLegs not guaranteed simultaneous
Advanced / Platform-Specific Orders
4 types
Spread Order
SPREAD
Advanced
Simultaneously buy one contract and sell another related contract (e.g., Gold Oct vs Gold Dec on MCX). Executes on the price difference between the two legs, not absolute price. Used for calendar spreads and arbitrage.
ExampleBuy MCX Gold Oct ₹100, Sell Gold Dec ₹102
Trade onThe ₹2 spread, not the legs
Margin benefitYes — reduced as legs offset risk
PlatformsMCX spread contracts, Zerodha, Dhan
✓ Lower margin than two outright positions
Calendar/Basis spreadLower marginMCX mainly
Margin Trade Facility
MTF
Advanced
Buy stocks/ETFs with leverage and carry the position for days/weeks by paying interest. Not technically a separate order type — it's a funding mechanism. Your broker pledges shares; you pay ~0.04–0.06% per day interest.
LeverageUp to 4–5x on eligible stocks
Carry cost~14–18% p.a. (broker-specific)
Margin call riskYes — if value drops, top-up required
PlatformsmStock (0% MTF), Kotak, HDFC Sec
⚠ Daily interest erodes returns if position held too long
Multi-day leverageInterest cost dailyEquity only (not F&O)
Synthetic Bracket
SYN-BO
Advanced
Manually replicating Bracket Order logic where native BO is unavailable (e.g., options, or platforms without BO). Entry filled → separately place SLM + Target Limit with manual cancel-the-other logic via API or screen.
EntryAny order type at ₹100
Post-fillManually place SLM ₹90 + Limit ₹130
Cancel logicManual or API webhook on fill alert
PlatformsAPI (Zerodha, Dhan, Fyers, AngelOne)
⚠ Requires discipline or automation — human error risk
Manual OCOWorks for options tooNo native auto-cancel
Beyond-Target Trail
BTT / Algo
Advanced
Your "leading jump" concept: after price crosses ₹130 target, don't exit — instead switch to trail mode with ₹10 jumps. Native SUPER Order exits at target; this requires algo override via API to continue trailing beyond ₹130.
At ₹130Cancel target exit, activate trail
Trail firesOnly if price drops ₹10 from peak
At ₹150 peakExits at ₹140 — ₹40 profit
ImplementationAPI (Python/Kite API) + alert webhook
⚠ Requires algo/API — not possible on any native platform UI
Unlimited upside captureAPI onlyCustom logic needed
Algo / Institutional Orders
3 types
VWAP Order
VWAP
Algo
Algorithm slices a large order into smaller child orders, targeting execution near the Volume Weighted Average Price over the day. Used by mutual funds / FIIs to minimize market impact on large trades.
GoalAverage fill ≈ day's VWAP
MethodSmall slices sent throughout session
Use caseLarge qty — 500+ lots
PlatformsInstitutional OMS, Symphony Presto, API
ℹ Retail traders can approximate manually via spaced limit orders
Minimizes market impactInstitutional/API only
TWAP Order
TWAP
Algo
Algorithm splits a large order into equal time-interval slices. E.g., buy 100 lots: place 10 lots every 30 minutes over 5 hours. Time-based rather than volume-based like VWAP. Simpler but less adaptive.
GoalAverage fill over time, ignore volume
MethodEqual qty every fixed time slice
Use casePredictable execution scheduling
PlatformsAPI / institutional OMS
ℹ Simpler than VWAP — predictable but ignores liquidity windows
Predictable scheduleAPI onlyIgnores volume
API Custom Algo Order
ALGO / API
Algo
Any order logic you write yourself via broker API (Zerodha Kite, Dhan API, Fyers API). Enables trailing stop beyond targets, dynamic SL shifts on indicator signals, multi-leg hedging, and fully automated strategy execution.
LanguagesPython (most popular), Node.js, Java
Order types availableAll exchange-supported types
Key capabilityConditional → react to fills, prices, time
PlatformsZerodha Kite, Dhan, Fyers, AngelOne API
✓ Unlimited logic — most powerful trading tool available
Unlimited logicAll order typesRequires coding

Quick Decision Guide
I need guaranteed exit, no matter what price
→ Stop-Loss Market (SLM)
I want exact entry price with patience
→ Limit Order + GTT
I want to ride the trend and lock profit
→ Trailing Stop Loss / SUPER Order
I want full automation (entry + SL + target)
→ Bracket Order or SUPER Order
I want lowest possible margin
→ Cover Order (then Bracket Order)
I want to enter weeks later at a specific price
→ GTT (Good Till Triggered)
I want SL to auto-activate when entry fills
→ OTO / SUPER Order / API Algo
I want to trail BEYOND my target (₹130+)
→ API Algo / Beyond-Target Trail
I want price floor but accept no-fill risk
→ Stop-Loss Limit (SL-L)
I want to buy multiple stocks simultaneously
→ Basket Order
I'm placing order tonight for tomorrow
→ After Market Order (AMO)
I'm an institution moving 500+ lots quietly
→ VWAP / Iceberg / Disclosed Qty
