01 —

The Global Gold Market — Size & Structure

Gold is not just a commodity. It is simultaneously a monetary metal, a safe-haven asset, an industrial input, and a cultural symbol. Understanding its market requires seeing all four dimensions at once.

Economics Professor Perspective

Above-Ground Stock

~212,000 tonnes total ever mined. ~190,000 tonnes above ground today. Annual mine output ~3,300–3,600 tonnes. The entire above-ground stock would fill roughly 3.5 Olympic swimming pools.

Demand Composition

Jewellery ~45–50% (India + China dominate). Investment 25–30% (bars, coins, ETFs). Central banks ~15% (net buying since 2010). Technology ~7–8% (electronics, dentistry).

India's Position

World's 2nd largest gold consumer (~800–900 tonnes/year import + domestic recycling). Gold imports ~2–3% of India's total import bill. Household gold holdings estimated at 25,000+ tonnes — larger than most central bank reserves globally.

Market Size

LBMA daily OTC turnover ~$50–130 billion. COMEX daily volume ~30–60 million troy oz equivalent. MCX daily turnover ~₹5,000–15,000 crore (gold). OTC market is 10-20× larger than all exchanges combined.

Economics Professor — Gold as Money

Why Gold is Special — Economic Properties

  • Scarcity: ~186,000 tonnes above ground. Annual production adds only ~1.7% to total stock — the lowest stock-to-flow ratio of any commodity except silver
  • Durability: Does not corrode, rust, or degrade. Gold coins from 2,000 years ago are still pure gold
  • Fungibility: 1oz of 99.9% gold anywhere = 1oz of 99.9% gold everywhere
  • Divisibility: Can be refined to any size without losing value proportionally
  • Portability: High value-to-weight ratio
  • Universal acceptance: No counterparty required — unlike currency, requires no government backing to be valued

Gold vs Inflation — The Real Story

Gold is commonly called an "inflation hedge" but the academic evidence is nuanced:

  • Long run (decades): Gold broadly preserves real purchasing power. ₹1 worth of gold in 1947 buys roughly the same goods today as ₹1 would have then, adjusted.
  • Short to medium run: Gold can significantly underperform inflation for extended periods (1980–2000 gold fell ~70% in real terms)
  • Real yields: The strongest predictor of gold is real interest rates (nominal rate − inflation). When real yields are negative → gold thrives. Positive real yields → gold struggles
  • Dollar correlation: Gold has ~−0.6 to −0.8 correlation with the DXY dollar index over most rolling 5-year periods
Professor's Note — Gold is Not a Commodity in the Classical Sense

True commodities are consumed. Oil is burned. Wheat is eaten. Cotton is woven. Gold is accumulated. The gold in Fort Knox today might have been mined by the Romans. This makes gold's price dynamics fundamentally different — it responds to changes in the desire to hold existing stock, not just marginal production costs. This is why gold can move 20% on a change in Fed language while copper barely moves.

   ÷ 31.1035 (grams per troy oz)

COMEX Trader — Commitment of Traders (COT) Report

Every Friday, CFTC (US regulator) publishes the COT report — a breakdown of positions held by three categories: Commercials (hedgers — mostly short, natural sellers like miners/producers), Non-Commercials (large speculators — hedge funds, CTA funds), and Non-Reportable (small retail). The Non-Commercial net long/short position is the single most useful institutional sentiment indicator for gold globally. When large specs are extremely net long → contrarian bearish warning. When they're near max net short → contrarian bullish setup. Published at: cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders

Contract Mechanics — How It Actually Works

Margins, MTM, P&L calculation, rollover, delivery — the operational plumbing of futures trading.

Research Analyst — Margin System

Margin Types on MCX

  • SPAN Margin (Initial): The minimum margin required to open a position, set by MCX using the SPAN (Standard Portfolio Analysis of Risk) model. Changes daily based on volatility. Roughly 4–6% of contract value for Gold.
  • Exposure Margin: Additional margin over SPAN. Typically 1–2% of contract value. Acts as a buffer.
  • Total Initial Margin = SPAN + Exposure. Your broker may charge higher than this minimum.
  • Maintenance Margin: If your account falls below this (typically 75% of initial), you get a margin call — top up or positions get squared off.
  • MTM (Mark to Market): Daily settlement. Profits credited, losses debited from your account at end of each session based on settlement price (usually VWAP of last 30 minutes).
Trader — P&L Mechanics

P&L Calculation — Gold Mini Example

// Gold Mini: 100g lot, quoted per 10g

Profit/Loss = (Exit Price − Entry Price)
            ÷ 10 × Lot Size in grams

// Example: Buy 1 lot at ₹71,000/10g
// Sell 1 lot at ₹72,500/10g

P&L = (72,500 − 71,000) / 10 × 100
     = ₹15,000 gross profit

// Each ₹1 move in price per 10g = ₹10 P&L (Gold Mini)
// Each ₹1 move in price per 10g = ₹100 P&L (Gold 1kg)

Rollover — How to Avoid Delivery

  • What: Closing your position in the expiring month and simultaneously opening the same position in the next month's contract
  • When to roll: MCX Gold: 5–7 days before expiry. Liquidity starts migrating to next month. Spreads widen in near-month.
  • Rollover cost = spread between near and far month. Usually ₹100–400 per 10g (reflects carrying cost + interest + storage)
  • Contango: Far month > Near month (normal). You pay to roll — carry cost.
  • Backwardation: Near month > Far month. Rare in gold. You receive money to roll — signals physical shortage.
  • Watch MCX rollover data published on their website — shows when bulk rolling is happening.

Stop Loss Types on MCX

  • Market SL: Triggers at your price, executes at market. Can have slippage in illiquid hours.
  • Limit SL (SL-L): Triggers at your stop price, places a limit order. Risk: if market gaps past your limit, order may not execute.
  • SL-Market (SL-M): Triggers at stop price, executes as market order. Better for liquid contracts. Most practical for Gold Mini.
  • Trailing SL: Moves with price in your favour. Available on Dhan, Zerodha. Useful for trend-following on gold.
  • Good Till Triggered (GTT): Persistent stop orders that survive session end. Critical for overnight MCX positions.
Trader's Key Point — MTM Can Wipe You Without a Big Move

Because MCX gold contracts are margined at ~5% of contract value, a 5% move against you wipes your entire margin. Gold Mini at ₹72,000/10g with ₹9,000 margin: a move of ₹900 per 10g = ₹9,000 loss on 100g = entire margin gone. That's only a 1.25% price move. Always size positions so that a 3× expected daily range move doesn't exceed 2% of your total capital.

Economics / Tax — Income Tax Treatment

Income Tax Treatment — Commodity Futures India

Classification — Non-Speculative Business Income

Under Section 43(5) of the Income Tax Act, commodity futures are explicitly classified as non-speculative business income — unlike equity intraday trading which is speculative.

This is a critical distinction:

  • Non-speculative losses can be set off against any other business income in the same year
  • Carry forward: Losses can be carried forward for 8 years and set off against non-speculative income only (not salary)
  • Speculative losses (equity intraday) can only be set off against speculative profits — much more restrictive

Tax Rates & Compliance

  • Rate: Taxed at applicable slab rate (not flat %). No special rate for commodity futures like LTCG/STCG for equities
  • ITR Form: ITR-3 (business income with capital gains) or ITR-4 (presumptive). NOT ITR-2.
  • Books of accounts: Mandatory if turnover > ₹1 crore. Note: turnover = sum of absolute values of all profits + losses (not gross contract value)
  • Tax audit (Section 44AB): Required if turnover > ₹10 crore, or if claiming losses and turnover > ₹1 crore
  • Advance tax: Pay quarterly if expected tax liability > ₹10,000. Miss this → 1% per month interest under 234B/234C
  • GST registration: Not required for individuals trading their own account

Open Interest — Deep Analysis

Open Interest is the most misunderstood and most powerful indicator in futures markets. It tells you not what price is doing, but who is doing it and with how much conviction.

COMEX Trader — OI Fundamentals

What OI Actually Is

The total number of futures contracts that are currently open — not yet closed, expired, or delivered. Every open contract has exactly one long and one short. OI = 50,000 means 50,000 longs AND 50,000 shorts exist simultaneously.

OI Update Frequency

Intraday OI on your broker terminal is batched — updates every few minutes, not per trade. The clearing house must net all transactions to determine true OI. EOD OI (published post-market) is the accurate, reliable figure. Always use EOD OI for analysis.

OI vs Volume Key Difference

Volume resets to zero every day — counts every trade. OI is cumulative — only changes when positions open or close. A day with high volume but flat OI means traders are churning existing positions. High volume + rising OI = genuine new commitment.

What Drives Unexplained OI Changes

  • Bullion bank delta rebalancing: Options book exposure changes → algo adjusts futures hedge. Happens continuously, algorithmically. No news, just mechanics.
  • Jeweller batch hedging: Weekly/monthly reconciliation of physical gold sold. Sudden OI moves on quiet Mondays often this.
  • Import hedge adjustment: Nominated banks (SBI, MMTC) adjust hedges after large import consignments clear customs.
  • Fund rebalancing: ETFs, gold funds rebalance when AUM changes (inflows/outflows).
  • Rollover: OI shifts between near and far month ~7–10 days before expiry. Not new positions — just migration.
Advanced OI Concept — Open Interest Concentration

SEBI publishes OI concentration data — what % of total OI is held by top 3 / top 5 participants. If top 3 clients hold 60%+ of OI on one side, the market is concentrated and vulnerable to a squeeze. Watch for this near expiry. When a large player holds a dominant short and is unable to deliver, they may need to buy back at any price — creating a short squeeze. Conversely, a concentrated long near expiry with delivery threat can force shorts to cover. These dynamics occasionally play out in Indian commodity markets.

Volume Analysis

Volume is the fuel of price moves. A price move without volume is suspicious. A price move with exceptional volume is significant. Volume precedes price — know what to look for.

COMEX Trader — Volume Principles

Volume in Lots vs Grams

MCX volume is quoted in lots, not grams. To convert:

• Gold Petal: 1 lot = 1g
• Gold Guinea: 1 lot = 8g
• Gold Mini: 1 lot = 100g
• Gold (1kg): 1 lot = 1,000g

Always mentally anchor to grams when comparing volume across contracts. Gold Mini volume 10,000 = 1,000kg. Gold 1kg volume 10,000 = 10,000kg. Completely different scale.

When Volume Spikes — What to Look For

  • US economic data: CPI, PCE, NFP, FOMC — volume spikes 5–10× normal in the 7–11 PM IST window
  • Geopolitical events: War escalation, sanctions — sudden overnight volume surge at MCX open
  • RBI / SEBI announcements: Import duty changes, margin changes — volume spike in Indian session
  • Gold ETF flow data: Large GLD/IAU inflows/outflows published weekly — triggers volume repositioning
  • COMEX option expiry: Quarterly option expiry creates heavy volume as dealers delta-hedge
  • Rollover week: Volume in near month declines, far month surges

VWAP in Gold Trading

Volume Weighted Average Price — the average price weighted by volume traded at each level.

  • Institutional benchmark — large orders try to execute near VWAP to minimize market impact
  • Price above VWAP intraday = buyers dominant
  • Price below VWAP intraday = sellers dominant
  • MCX Gold settlement price (last 30-min VWAP) is the daily MTM reference — this is why volume often spikes in the last 30 minutes of session as participants try to influence settlement
  • On Dhan / Zerodha, VWAP is visible as a chart indicator — use it on 5-min charts during US session

Volume Patterns That Matter

  • Exhaustion volume: Price makes a new high/low on very high volume but fails to hold — suggests the last buyers/sellers have entered. Reversal signal.
  • Accumulation volume: Price flat or slightly down but volume consistently high — suggests large players are absorbing sell orders. Bullish beneath the surface.
  • Distribution volume: Price flat or slightly up but volume consistently high — large players selling into strength.
  • Dry-up volume: Price consolidates on very low volume — coiling spring. Often precedes breakout in either direction.
  • Confirmation volume: Breakout from consolidation with volume 2–3× average — genuine move, not a false break.

OI + Volume Combined Signal Matrix

The combination of price direction, OI change, and volume creates a diagnostic framework for understanding market conviction. Here is every meaningful combination.

PRICE ↑ OI ↑ Strong Bull

New longs entering aggressively. Fresh money backing the move. Both buyers AND sellers are opening new positions — sellers expect to profit short-term, buyers are more aggressive. Trend has structural conviction. The strongest signal for trend continuation. Look for entry on pullbacks.

PRICE ↑ OI ↓ Short Covering

Shorts panicking and buying back. No new longs entering — existing shorts are forced to exit. Rally is mechanically driven, not fundamentally driven. Do not chase this move. Once shorts finish covering, price may stall or reverse. Check if there's a known catalyst for the covering.

PRICE ↓ OI ↑ Strong Bear

New shorts entering with conviction. Bears are building positions, not just longs exiting. Downtrend has structural backing. The mirror of "Strong Bull." Strong continuation signal. Fresh short sellers see value in shorting at current levels. Avoid buying dips until this signal reverses.

PRICE ↓ OI ↓ Trend Exhaustion

Longs cutting losses and exiting. No new sellers — existing longs are liquidating. No conviction behind the fall. Once long liquidation is complete, selling pressure dries up. Potential reversal zone. Look for price stabilisation and OI to stop falling before entering long.

The Jeweller Hedging Reality

  • Gold Metal Loans: Most large jewellers don't own their inventory — they lease gold from bullion banks at 1–3% annual lease rate. Lower upfront capital than buying outright.
  • Forward sales: Large jewellers with Akshaya Tritiya/Dhanteras advance bookings go LONG futures to lock procurement price — not short
  • Daily reconciliation: Tanishq/Kalyan likely have treasury desks doing T+1 reconciliation. Smaller chains — weekly at best
  • The Monday OI effect: Look for unexplained OI shifts on Monday morning — often jewellers adjusting hedges for the previous week's sales data

Sovereign Gold Bonds — The RBI Instrument

  • Issued by RBI, backed by GOI. Denominated in grams.
  • Interest: 2.5% per annum (semi-annual) on issue price
  • Capital gains: Tax-free if held to 8-year maturity
  • Can be used as collateral for loans
  • Secondary market trading on NSE/BSE — but liquidity is poor
  • Issue price = RBI set (average of previous week's IBJA closing price for 999 gold)
  • Not a futures instrument — but competes with GOLDBEES and physical gold as an investment vehicle
  • No making charges, no storage risk unlike physical

Bullion Banks & Refineries

These institutions form the backbone of the global gold market. They operate at scale and sophistication that retail participants rarely appreciate — and their behaviour drives market structure.

Research Analyst — Institutional Gold Infrastructure

What Makes a Bullion Bank

Specifically licensed to deal in physical gold at wholesale scale. Not just financial intermediaries — they have physical gold vaulting infrastructure, refinery relationships, and direct central bank access.

  • Global: HSBC, JP Morgan, UBS, Citigroup, Scotia Bank (ScotiaMocatta), ICBC Standard
  • India: SBI, HDFC Bank, Axis Bank, Kotak Mahindra, ICICI (all have bullion divisions)
  • They are LBMA members — the global club
  • They hold "unallocated gold" accounts for clients — gold by book entry, no specific bars assigned

Gold Metal Loans — How India Runs on Leased Gold

  • Bullion bank imports gold → lends to jeweller
  • Jeweller pays gold lease rate (~1–3% annually) — not the full gold price upfront
  • Jeweller manufactures, sells jewellery, repays gold (or cash equivalent) to bank
  • Bank holds the price risk — hedges on futures
  • This system means India's jewellery industry is essentially leverage-financed by bullion banks
  • When gold lease rates spike → working capital cost for jewellers rises → they increase making charges or reduce promotions
  • Lease rate is set in London OTC — another LBMA price that affects Indian market

MMTC-PAMP — India's Key Refinery

  • Located in Haryana (Manesar)
  • LBMA accredited — produces Good Delivery bars
  • MCX approved delivery — only LBMA / MCX-approved refineries qualify for MCX Gold (1kg) delivery

India Import Mechanics

  • Import duty: ~15% currently (basic + AIDC + GST component) — subject to Budget changes. This is why MCX premium to COMEX exists.
  • Grey channel (smuggling): When duty is high, smuggling increases, suppressing MCX premium
  • KYC on import: Banks must report gold imports above threshold to RBI
  • Star Trading Houses: Eligible to import gold without MMTC/SBI intermediation
The LBMA Unallocated Gold System — How Paper Gold Works

Most gold "held" at bullion banks is unallocated — you own a claim on gold, not specific bars in a vault. The bank can lend this gold to others (fractional reserve style). Allocated gold = specific numbered bars in a segregated vault with your name. Unallocated = cheaper (no storage fee) but you're an unsecured creditor to the bank. In a systemic crisis (2008-style), unallocated gold claims could be at risk. This is why central banks increasingly demand repatriation of allocated gold (Germany, Turkey, India moved gold back from London/NY).

  • Government scheme to mobilise household gold into productive use

  • Gold is melted, used by jewellers / banks
  • Largely unsuccessful — only a few tonnes mobilised vs 25,000+ tonne household stock
  • Cultural resistance: gold as family asset, not investment
  • Signifies government awareness of India's "gold problem" — huge capital locked up unproductively

OTC Markets, Grey Areas & Information Asymmetry

The most important gold price action happens in markets you cannot see. Understanding the OTC layer and its relationship to MCX/COMEX is essential for not being perpetually surprised.

LBMA — The Real Benchmark

London Bullion Market Association. The apex of global gold price discovery.

  • Daily AM Fix (~3:00 PM IST) and PM Fix (~7:30 PM IST)
  • Fix determined by electronic auction among LBMA members
  • Used as settlement reference in mining contracts, long-term supply deals, central bank transactions
  • LBMA Good Delivery list = the quality standard for internationally traded gold bars
  • ~$50–130B daily OTC volume — dwarfs all futures exchanges
  • India's MCX price is ultimately derived from LBMA/COMEX

IBJA — India's Daily Reference

  • India Bullion and Jewellers Association, Mumbai
  • Sets morning (~10:30 AM) and evening (~5:30 PM) reference prices
  • Used by all wholesale physical transactions — jewellers, banks, refineries
  • Rate = COMEX spot converted to INR + import duty + handling charges + margins
  • A handful of large members negotiate the rate — entirely OTC, no exchange transparency
  • IBJA rate can diverge from MCX price (premium/discount) depending on physical demand-supply
  • Website: ibja.co — check rates daily if you trade MCX gold

Zaveri Bazaar — Physical Centre

  • Mumbai's historic gold market — centuries old
  • Billions in physical gold traded daily via phone, WhatsApp, in-person
  • Relationships and reputation = capital here
  • Price is IBJA referenced but negotiated bilaterally
  • Zaveri Bazaar premium/discount to IBJA reflects real-time physical demand in India
  • During high-demand periods (Akshaya Tritiya, Dhanteras) — premium spikes
  • During post-Budget or duty-hike periods — discount forms as demand drops
  • This premium/discount is a real-time indicator of Indian physical demand — more honest than MCX
Known Structural Opacity & Grey Areas

These are not conspiracy theories — they are documented structural realities of how Indian and global gold markets function:

  • Policy front-running: Import duty changes, LTV cap changes, RBI gold purchase announcements — players with government connections position before public disclosure. Technically illegal under SEBI insider trading rules, but commodity market rules are less stringent than equity markets.
  • IBJA rate management: When a handful of large members set the reference price privately, scope for coordination exists. SEBI and CCI have had this on their radar.
  • Bullion bank front-running client orders: Executing a large central bank purchase creates knowledge of upcoming demand — positions taken before client execution are front-running. Policed in equities, very weakly in OTC bullion.
  • COMEX manipulation allegations: JP Morgan paid $1 billion+ fine in 2020 to settle US DOJ charges of precious metals market manipulation (spoofing). Traders placed and cancelled large orders to create false price signals.
  • NSEL fraud (2013): ₹5,500 crore. Fabricated warehouse receipts. Jignesh Shah arrested. Led to SEBI absorbing FMC's role. The most spectacular commodity exchange fraud in India. Research this thoroughly — understanding what went wrong is essential for understanding what safeguards now exist.

What Actually Moves Gold Price

In order of historical influence:

Economic Theory of Gold

From a professor's perspective — the intellectual framework for understanding why gold behaves as it does, and why even professional economists disagree on its fundamental nature.

Economics Professor — Gold Theory

Gold Standard History — Brief

  • Classical Gold Standard (1870–1914): Most major currencies pegged to gold. International trade settled in gold. Constrained government spending. Deflation-prone.
  • Bretton Woods (1944–1971): USD pegged to gold at $35/oz. Other currencies pegged to USD. US held global gold reserves at Fort Knox.
  • Nixon Shock (Aug 15, 1971): Nixon unilaterally suspended dollar-gold convertibility. Gold became a free-floating commodity. This is why gold went from $35 to $800 in 8 years.
  • Post-1971: All fiat currencies. Central banks hold gold as reserves not as monetary basis but as store of value / confidence anchor.
  • Implication: Everything since 1971 — gold's price is a measure of confidence in fiat currencies. Gold at $2,500 = the dollar has lost 98.6% of its purchasing power relative to gold since 1971.

Gibson's Paradox and Gold

Pre-1971, gold and interest rates moved together (both reflected inflation). Post-1971, the relationship inverted — gold now moves inverse to real rates. This is because:

  • Gold's opportunity cost = the real yield on holding bonds vs gold
  • When real yields rise → bonds more attractive → gold sold
  • When real yields fall / go negative → gold is better than "losing money in real terms on bonds"
  • 2020–2021: Real yields hit −1% → Gold hit all-time highs of $2,075
  • 2022: Fed raised rates sharply → Real yields went +2% → Gold underperformed
  • 2023–2024: Gold broke the "inverse real yield" relationship — rose even as real yields stayed high → suggests structural demand from central banks and China overwhelmed the rate narrative

Stock-to-Flow Model

Popularised for Bitcoin but applies to gold first. SF = Total above-ground stock ÷ Annual new production.

  • Gold SF ~58 (58 years of current production = existing stock)
  • Silver SF ~22
  • Oil SF ~1.5 (consumed)
  • High SF = price insensitive to new supply shocks — even doubling mine output barely dents SF
  • This is why gold isn't destroyed by supply increases the way oil would be
  • Bitcoin designed to mimic gold's SF model

Gold as Currency Insurance

Countries with sanctions risk or currency risk systematically buy gold:

  • Russia: After 2014 Crimea sanctions, doubled gold reserves. When SWIFT sanctions hit in 2022, gold exports kept economy partially functional.
  • China: Reducing USD Treasury holdings, buying gold. Hedging against dollar weaponisation.
  • India (RBI): Diversifying reserves — repatriated gold from Bank of England in 2024.
  • This de-dollarisation trade is the structural bid under gold since 2022 that has broken the normal real-yield relationship.

Gold in an Indian Portfolio — Theory

  • Gold has ~0.1–0.2 correlation with Nifty 50 over 10-year rolling periods — genuine diversifier
  • Indian gold price has currency built in — a rupee hedge as well as a gold exposure
  • Traditional Indian asset allocation: 10–15% gold (household standard — historically justified)
  • Gold in India = insurance against: currency devaluation, geopolitical disruption, government policy risk
  • Problem: Physical gold has wealth tax scrutiny risk, storage cost, no income
  • Solution matrix: GOLDBEES (liquid, taxable), SGB (tax-free, illiquid), MCX (levered, income-generating potential)

Trading Strategies — COMEX Trader Perspective

How professional gold traders actually approach markets — from scalping to macro position building. The framework matters more than specific entries.

COMEX Trader — Operational Framework

Time Horizon Framework

  • Scalping (mins–hours): Trade COMEX GC or MCX Gold Mini around US data releases. Focus on order flow, DOM (depth of market). Requires very fast execution. Not for beginners.
  • Intraday (hours): India session (9AM–5PM) or US session (7PM–11:30PM). Use VWAP, volume profile, OI intraday changes.
  • Swing (days–weeks): Combine OI analysis + daily chart structure + macro catalyst timeline. Best for MCX Gold Mini with GTT stop orders.
  • Positional (weeks–months): Based on real yield outlook, COT positioning, central bank demand cycle. Use MCX Gold (1kg) or GOLDBEES.
  • Strategic (months–years): SGB, physical, or GOLDBEES based on macro thesis.

Risk Management — Non-Negotiables

  • Position sizing: Maximum 1–2% of total capital risk per trade. On Gold Mini (₹9,000 margin), if stop is ₹500/10g away (= ₹5,000 loss), you need ₹2,50,000+ capital to risk 2%.
  • Overnight risk: Gold gaps on COMEX overnight regularly. Never hold overnight positions you can't afford to gap through. Use GTT stops as minimum protection.
  • Event risk: Remove or reduce exposure before: FOMC, US CPI, NFP, RBI policy, Union Budget. These events routinely create ₹500–2,000/10g gaps in minutes.
  • Correlation risk: If you hold both GOLDBEES + MCX long + SGB → you have concentrated gold exposure. Diversification within gold instruments is not diversification.
  • Rollover management: Set calendar reminders for MCX contract expiry. Rollover 5–7 days before to avoid thin liquidity and wide spreads in expiry week.

Key Trade Setups — Gold

Real Yield Trade

  • Setup: US 10Y TIPS yield turning negative / falling sharply
  • Entry: Long COMEX GC / MCX Gold Mini on first confirmed down-day in real yield
  • Time horizon: Weeks to months
  • Stop: Below technical support, or real yield reversal
  • Best catalyst: Fed pivot language, inflation data surprise
  • Data source: FRED (fred.stlouisfed.org) — search DFII10 for real yield

COT Extreme Positioning

  • Setup: Non-commercial net position at multi-year extreme (very long or very short)
  • Logic: When speculators are maximally positioned one way, they become the future sellers/buyers
  • Extreme net long → Contrarian short / avoid new longs
  • Extreme net short → Contrarian long setup — short squeeze potential
  • Time horizon: 2–6 weeks
  • COT data every Friday: cftc.gov

India Import Duty Event

  • Setup: Budget / pre-Budget period where duty change is speculated
  • Duty reduction → MCX premium compresses. Import parity shifts. Short the MCX vs COMEX spread.
  • Duty increase → MCX premium widens. Long MCX spot/futures.
  • July 2024 example: Duty cut from ~15% to ~6% → MCX crashed ₹5,000+/10g overnight
  • Signal: Track pre-Budget media speculation, jewellery industry lobby statements
What Most Retail Traders Get Wrong About Gold
  • Trading MCX without watching COMEX: You are not trading Indian gold — you are trading a COMEX derivative with INR translation. Ignoring COMEX is trading blind.
  • Ignoring USDINR: A flat COMEX night with a 1% rupee move = significant MCX move. Many retail traders are surprised by this.
  • Over-trading around US events: The bid-ask spread widens to 5–10× normal around FOMC/CPI releases on MCX. Your ₹20 brokerage is the least of your cost problems — slippage in that window can be ₹100–200/10g.
  • Not accounting for rollover cost: Every contract cycle has a carry cost. If you're perpetually long gold via MCX rollovers, you're paying the carry — this can be ₹150–400/10g per month depending on the curve. GOLDBEES avoids this problem.
  • Treating OI changes as actionable in real-time: Intraday OI is approximated. EOD is reliable. Never trade based on intraday OI alone.
