07 · Regulatory & Tax Notes

// Key Context for MCX Gold Traders

  • CTT = 0.01% on SELL side only, introduced July 2013. Non-deductible for salaried individuals. Paid on every exit, profit or loss.
  • Income Tax — MCX futures P&L is non-speculative business income under Section 43(5). Taxed at full slab rate — not capital gains.
  • Losses are deductible — set off against other business income (not salary). Carry forward up to 8 years.
  • GST 18% applies only to brokerage and MCX exchange transaction charges — not on CTT, stamp duty, or SEBI fees.
  • Stamp Duty = 0.002% on buy side, Central Stamp Duty effective June 2020.
  • Scalping MCX Gold is structurally unprofitable — min viable move after all friction is ₹300+/10g. Trade multi-day swings, not noise.
  • SEBI turnover fee = ₹10 per crore of total turnover (both sides combined).
  • Net P&L on ₹0 move = negative because CTT + all charges still apply even if price doesn't move.
