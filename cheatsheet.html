06 — OI + PRICE SIGNAL MATRIX
PRICE ↑OI ↑STRONG BULL

Fresh longs entering with conviction. New money backing move. Trend continuation signal. Enter on pullbacks.

PRICE ↑OI ↓SHORT COVER

Shorts forced to buy back. No new bulls. Don't chase. Rally may stall once covering ends.

PRICE ↓OI ↑STRONG BEAR

Fresh shorts entering. Bears building with conviction. Avoid buying dips. Downtrend has legs.

PRICE ↓OI ↓EXHAUSTION

Longs liquidating. No new sellers. Fall losing steam. Potential reversal zone — wait for OI to stabilise.

09 — COMMON MISTAKES (DON'T DO THESE)
Trading MCX without watching COMEX + USDINR. You're trading a COMEX derivative with INR translation. Ignoring either is trading blind.
Holding unhedged overnight before US events. FOMC/CPI/NFP routinely create ₹500–2,000/10g gaps in minutes. Use GTT stops minimum.
Trading intraday OI as fact. Intraday OI is approximated and batched. Only EOD OI is reliable for analysis.
Not accounting for rollover cost. Each month you roll MCX = ₹150–400/10g carry cost. GOLDBEES has zero rollover cost.
Filing ITR-2 for futures income. Commodity futures = non-speculative business. Must use ITR-3.

13 — ECONOMIC THEORY QUICK REFERENCE
Real Yield Rule
  • Real yield = Nominal rate − Inflation
  • Negative real yield → Gold thrives
  • Positive & rising real yield → Gold struggles
  • 2020–21: Real yield −1% → Gold $2,075 ATH
  • 2022: Fed hikes → Real yield +2% → Gold underperformed
  • 2023–24: Gold broke pattern — central bank buying overrode rates
Gold Standard History
  • 1870–1914: Classical Gold Standard
  • 1944–1971: Bretton Woods — USD = $35/oz
  • Aug 15, 1971: Nixon ends convertibility
  • Gold: $35 → $800 in 8 years after Nixon Shock
  • Post-1971: Gold = confidence in fiat currencies
  • Gold at $2,500 = dollar lost ~98% vs gold since 1971
Stock-to-Flow + De-dollarisation
  • Gold SF ratio ~58 (years of supply = existing stock)
  • High SF = price insensitive to supply shocks
  • Russia: doubled gold reserves post-2014 sanctions
  • China: reducing UST, buying gold
  • India: repatriated gold from Bank of England 2024
  • De-dollarisation trade = structural gold bid
